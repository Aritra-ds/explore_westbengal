Explore Hooghly

A district with rich colonial history and scenic beauty.

History of Hooghly

Hooghly is one of the oldest districts in West Bengal, with a significant colonial history. It was once a thriving port city under British rule and played a key role in the trade routes connecting India to the rest of the world.

The district is home to several historical landmarks, including old churches, temples, and colonial-era structures that stand as a testament to its rich cultural and architectural legacy.

Tourist Attractions

Hooghly Imambara – A prominent historical mosque with intricate architecture.

Bandel Church – A historic Catholic church known for its stunning Portuguese architecture.

Chandannagar – A city with French colonial influence, featuring beautiful riverfronts.

Hooghly Riverfront – A scenic spot along the river, perfect for leisurely walks and breathtaking sunsets.

Serampore College – One of the oldest missionary colleges in India with colonial architecture.

Cultural Heritage

Hooghly is known for its rich artistic traditions, including folk music and dance.

Historical Landmarks

Hooghly is home to historic sites like the Dutch Cemetery and Chinsurah's colonial-era architecture.

Natural Beauty

Hooghly boasts scenic landscapes, serene river views, and lush greenery.
