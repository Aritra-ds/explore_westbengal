A land of scenic beauty and tribal culture.

History of Purulia

Purulia is a district located in the western part of West Bengal, known for its picturesque landscapes, dense forests, and tribal culture. The district is famous for its cultural richness, especially its association with the traditional Chhau dance, a masked dance form that originated in the region.

Purulia also has a historical connection with the British era, during which it was a strategic area. The district has many ancient temples, fortresses, and other historical landmarks, reflecting the region’s legacy.

Top Places to Visit in Purulia

Turga Falls – A mesmerizing waterfall nestled in the Ajodhya Hills, ideal for nature enthusiasts and trekkers.

Ajodhya Hills – A stunning range of hills offering panoramic views, trekking trails, and tribal cultural insights.

Marble Lake – A beautiful and hidden lake with turquoise water formed from an old quarry, surrounded by hills.

District Science Centre – An engaging space that promotes science education through interactive exhibits and models.

Subhash Park – A peaceful urban park in Purulia town, perfect for family outings and relaxation amidst greenery.

Culture

Chhau Dance is a vibrant masked art form of Purulia, blending martial arts, folklore, and tribal traditions.

Traditional Crafts

The region is known for its beautiful handmade crafts, including tribal art and textiles.

Heritage Sites

The district is rich in heritage, with ancient temples and ruins that tell the stories of its glorious past.
