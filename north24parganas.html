History of North 24 Parganas

North 24 Parganas is one of West Bengal’s most populous and culturally diverse districts. Originally part of the ancient Bengal delta, the region evolved significantly during the colonial and post-independence periods.

It blends bustling urban areas like Barasat, Salt Lake, and Dum Dum with vast rural stretches and eco-sensitive zones, forming a vital link between Kolkata and the Indo-Bangladesh border.

Tourist Attractions

Dakshineswar Temple – Sacred temple on the Ganges, linked with Ramakrishna Paramhansa.

Baranagar Museum – A treasure trove of Bengal's ancient sculptures and artifacts.

Biswa Bangla Gate – Iconic skywalk symbolizing the spirit of modern and vibrant Bengal.

Bibhutibhushan Avayaranya – A serene forest known for its rich biodiversity offering a perfect escape into nature.

Barasat Park – A serene local getaway showcasing greenery and peaceful walkways.

Diverse Communities

North 24 Parganas is home to people of many faiths and cultures, coexisting in harmony.

Urban Connectivity

Well-connected through roadways, railways, and the metro — linking key Kolkata suburbs.

Nature & Biodiversity

The lush wetlands near the Sundarbans provide vital habitats for diverse wildlife and thriving ecosystems.
