A blend of beaches, temples, and rich culture.

History of Purba Medinipur

Purba Medinipur is a district located on the southeastern coast of West Bengal. It is renowned for its historical significance and picturesque beaches. The district is famous for its connection to ancient temples, such as the famous *Digha*, a popular tourist destination known for its serene beaches and rich history.

The region has been a witness to several cultural and political movements throughout history and played a role in the independence struggle. Purba Medinipur’s cultural and artistic heritage is reflected in its festivals, traditional music, and dance forms.

Tourist Attractions

Digha – A popular seaside town known for its calm beaches.

Tamluk – A historic town with ancient temples and archaeological sites.

Mandarmani – A peaceful beach destination perfect for relaxation.

Chandaneswar Temple is a revered Shiva temple near the Odisha border.

Bakkhali – A calm coastal town with pristine beaches and tranquil waters.

Marine Life & Fishing Culture

Fishing is a major livelihood here, reflecting the region’s coastal identity.

Beaches of Purba Medinipur

The district is home to some of the most beautiful beaches in West Bengal, such as Digha and Mandarmani.

Heritage Sites

Purba Medinipur houses a number of historical sites, including ancient temples, forts, and colonial-era buildings.
