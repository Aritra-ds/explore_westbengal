Explore Dakshin Dinajpur

A district rich in folk culture, riverine beauty, and tradition.

History of Dakshin Dinajpur

Dakshin Dinajpur was carved out of the erstwhile West Dinajpur district in 1992. It is a predominantly rural district, known for its scenic beauty and cultural traditions. Balurghat is the district headquarters and a center of art and literature.

The Atreyee River flows through this region, nurturing its fertile lands and cultural heritage. The district is also famous for folk dances like Gombhira and traditional crafts passed down through generations.

Tourist Attractions

Atreyee River – A lifeline of the district and a picturesque spot for reflection and serenity.

Balurghat Museum – Displays a rare collection of folk art, terracotta, and archaeological finds.

Kaldighi Park – Peaceful lake park offering boating, greenery, scenic views, and relaxation.

Bangarh Fort – Ancient archaeological site showcasing ruins of forts, temples, and history layers.

Banshihari – Known for its temple and natural setting, ideal for short spiritual retreats.

Traditional Crafts

Local artisans preserve heritage through clay pottery, weaving, and bamboo crafts.

Living Folk Traditions

Gombhira dance performances during festivals bring the community together in joy and reflection.

Scenic Countryside

Verdant fields, slow rivers, and rustic life offer a peaceful rural experience in Dakshin Dinajpur.
