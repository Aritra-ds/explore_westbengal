Explore Kalimpong

A peaceful hill station with monasteries, orchids, and Himalayan views.

History of Kalimpong

Kalimpong, located in the foothills of the eastern Himalayas, was once part of the Sikkimese and Bhutanese kingdoms before becoming part of British India in the 19th century. It served as a trade hub between India and Tibet, leading to its rich cultural blend.

Declared a separate district in 2017, Kalimpong is known for its educational institutions, colonial-era churches, monasteries, and flower nurseries, especially its world-famous orchids.

Tourist Attractions

Deolo Hill – The highest point in Kalimpong, offering Himalayan views and serenity.

Durpin Monastery – A calm monastery atop Durpin Hill with Tibetan murals and mountain views.

Cactus Nursery – One of the largest collections of cacti in Asia, attracting botanists and tourists.

Morgan House – A colonial mansion turned into a heritage hotel with Gothic charm.

Lepcha Museum – Showcasing the culture and heritage of the indigenous Lepcha community.

Orchid Paradise

Kalimpong’s nurseries export world-class orchids and flowers worldwide.

Colonial Elegance

Walk through colonial buildings and serene churches nestled in the hills.

Tranquility in the Hills

Known for its peaceful ambiance, Kalimpong is a perfect escape from city life.
