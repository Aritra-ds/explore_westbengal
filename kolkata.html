History of Kolkata

Kolkata, formerly known as Calcutta, served as the capital of British India until 1911. Its origins trace back to three villages — Sutanuti, Gobindapur, and Kalikata — merged by the British East India Company in the late 17th century. The city evolved into a vital port, a hub of colonial administration, and the birthplace of the Indian Renaissance.

During the 19th and early 20th centuries, Kolkata witnessed socio-political movements, educational reforms, and the emergence of iconic thinkers, revolutionaries, and artists. Today, it continues to reflect a blend of colonial charm, cultural richness, and intellectual legacy.

Tourist Attractions

Victoria Memorial – A majestic museum of colonial heritage.

Indian Museum – India’s oldest and largest museum of history.

Eco Park – An urban ecological park with diverse landscapes.

Dakshineswar Temple – Revered temple to Goddess Kali on the Ganges.

Science City – India’s largest science center blending education and fun.

Cultural Vibes

Kolkata hosts vibrant festivals like Durga Puja and a thriving art scene loved by all.

Flavours of Kolkata

From spicy street food to sweets like Rasgulla, every dish tells a story of taste.

Heritage Sites

Wander through colonial-era buildings echoing the city's rich British legacy.
