History of Bankura

Bankura is a district known for its rich cultural heritage, including the famous Bankura horse, and several temples and ruins from ancient times. The district is also known for its tribal culture and a wide array of natural landscapes, such as the lush forests of the Jhilimili and the beautiful hills of the Ajodhya Range.

Historically, Bankura was a part of the Malla dynasty's realm, and it played a key role in the spread of Hinduism through its temples. The district continues to thrive as a center of folklore, craft, and cultural festivities.

Tourist Attractions

Shushunia Hill – A popular destination for trekking, known for its stunning views and rocky terrain.

Jhilimili – A serene village surrounded by dense forests, perfect for nature lovers and relaxation.

Rash Macnha – A beautiful spot, known for its picturesque landscapes and tranquil atmosphere.

Madanmohan Temple – A historic temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, famous for its spiritual significance.

Mukutmanipur – A scenic reservoir town, known for its lush greenery and peaceful surroundings.

Architecture

Bankura features stunning temple designs with intricate terracotta carvings and historic charm.

Arts & Crafts

Bankura is known for its terracotta art and the unique Bankura horse craftwork.

Heritage Sites

The district is rich in heritage, with several historical temples and ancient ruins spread across the area.
