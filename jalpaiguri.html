Explore Jalpaiguri

A gateway to Dooars, tea gardens, forests, and rivers.

History of Jalpaiguri

Jalpaiguri, once part of the historic Kamrupa kingdom, has a legacy deeply tied to the British-era tea industry and the Bengal Dooars region. It has been an administrative hub since colonial times and plays a vital role in the socio-economic framework of North Bengal.

The district is known for its picturesque landscape featuring rivers like Teesta, forests like Gorumara, and heritage railways. The name Jalpaiguri is derived from 'Jalpai' (olive), indicating a region of olive trees.

Tourist Attractions

Gorumara National Park – A dense forest with rhinos, elephants, and a paradise for nature lovers.

Teesta River – A lifeline of the region, perfect for scenic views and adventure sports.

Jalpaiguri Rajbari – A historic royal residence echoing the architectural elegance of the region.

Dooars Tea Gardens – Vast plantations producing some of the finest tea in the world.

Jogomaya Temple – A revered spiritual site, drawing devotees with its serene, divine ambiance.

Legacy of Tea

The tea industry shapes the district’s economy and culture, with generations working on lush estates.

Wildlife Encounters

Gorumara and Chapramari forests are home to rhinos, bison, and deer, inviting eco-tourism.

Natural Beauty

From lush forests to flowing rivers, Jalpaiguri offers breathtaking natural landscapes.
