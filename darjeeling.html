History of Darjeeling

Darjeeling, nestled in the Himalayan foothills, was originally a part of the Kingdom of Sikkim and later came under British control in the 19th century. Its cool climate and scenic beauty made it a popular summer resort during the colonial era.

The British established tea plantations, which now produce the world-renowned Darjeeling Tea. The town also became an educational and cultural hub, housing prestigious schools and monasteries. Its heritage railway, rolling hills, and multicultural population add to its timeless charm.

Tourist Attractions

Tiger Hill – A mesmerizing sunrise point offering views of Kanchenjunga and Everest.

Darjeeling Himalayan Railway – A UNESCO heritage toy train ride through the hills.

Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoological Park – Home to rare Himalayan animals like red pandas.

**Peace Pagoda** – A serene symbol of peace with sweeping views of the surrounding hills.

Happy Valley Tea Estate – Tour the lush plantations of world-famous Darjeeling Tea.

Himalayan Culture

A blend of Tibetan, Nepali, and Bengali influences shine through music, dance, and tradition.

Darjeeling Cuisine

From steaming momos to thukpa and traditional teas, Darjeeling’s flavors reflect its culture.

Colonial & Monastic Heritage

Darjeeling is home to colonial landmarks and sacred monasteries like Ghoom and Bhutia Busty.
