History of Purba Bardhaman

Purba Bardhaman is rich in historical significance, marked by its association with the great Bengali Renaissance. The district is known for its cultural heritage, and it has also played a key role in the industrial development of the region.

From ancient temples to modern-day industries, Purba Bardhaman exemplifies the harmonious blend of tradition and progress. The district is home to several landmarks that tell stories of Bengal's glorious past.

Tourist Attractions

108 Shiv Mandir – Cluster of ancient temples in a unique formation.

Curzon Gate – Historic colonial structure and iconic city landmark.

Ramnabagan Wildlife Sanctuary – Forested home to diverse animal species.

Purbasthali Bird Sanctuary – Serene haven for migratory and native birds.

Attohas Temple – Ancient temple believed to be a Shakti Peetha site.

Agricultural Richness

Purba Bardhaman is known for its fertile soil, with rice, jute, and vegetables being major crops.

Architecture

Purba Bardhaman’s temples feature intricate carvings, domes, and traditional Bengali architectural styles.

Local Cuisine

Purba Bardhaman offers a rich array of Bengali cuisine, including sweets and traditional dishes.
