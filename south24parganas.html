A district of rich nature, history, and iconic landmarks.

History of South 24 Parganas

South 24 Parganas is known for its rich cultural heritage, including the Sundarbans mangrove forest, a UNESCO World Heritage site. The district has a diverse population, with many villages and coastal areas contributing to its unique cultural and ecological importance.

The district’s history also involves the struggle for independence, with many revolutionary movements taking root here, particularly in the early 20th century. The region is known for its natural beauty, wildlife, and the incredible river systems that define its geography.

Tourist Attractions

Sundarbans – The world’s largest mangrove forest, home to the Royal Bengal Tiger.

Sagar Island – A tranquil island with religious significance, famous for its temples.

Sonagaon – A historic village known for its ancient temples and rich cultural heritage.

Diamond Harbour – A scenic riverfront spot with historical importance in colonial times.

Mousuni Island – A serene and peaceful getaway with pristine beaches and abundant natural beauty.

Historical Landmarks

The district is home to remarkable historical landmarks, showcasing its rich architectural and cultural legacy.

Natural Beauty

Home to the Sunderbans and other natural wonders, the district is a haven for nature lovers.

Wildlife Adventures

Explore the diverse wildlife and rich ecosystems in the district's sanctuaries and national parks.
