The land of mangoes, silk, and ancient cities.

History of Malda

Malda, a historically rich district in North Bengal, has been a prominent cultural and trade center for centuries. It was once the capital of ancient Bengal under the Gour and Pandua dynasties. The district boasts magnificent ruins and architectural marvels.

Known for its mangoes and silk, Malda continues to thrive with its agricultural and handloom traditions, making it a unique blend of past glory and present vibrance.

Tourist Attractions

Gour – Ancient city ruins featuring Islamic architecture and history.

Adina Mosque – A historic site, once India’s largest mosque, now majestic.

Pandua – An archaeological site with relics from the Sultanate era.

Malda Museum – Preserving artifacts and history of Gour and Pandua.

Dakhil Darwaza – A historical gateway with Mughal architecture.

Famous Mangoes

Malda is known as the 'Mango City' of Bengal with delicious varieties like Fazli, Himsagar, and Langra.

Local Sweets & Cuisine

Malda is famous for sweets like Kansat, Roshokodombo, and seasonal treats.

Architectural Design

Malda showcases unique architectural designs blending cultural influences and history.
