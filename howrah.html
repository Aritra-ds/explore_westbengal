History of Howrah

Howrah, one of the oldest and most industrialized districts of West Bengal, stands on the western bank of the Hooghly River, opposite Kolkata. It has a rich colonial history tied closely with the British East India Company.

Known for its bustling trade routes and engineering feats like the iconic Howrah Bridge, the district evolved as an important railway and industrial hub. With time, it developed a strong cultural identity blending tradition and modernization.

Tourist Attractions

Howrah Bridge – Iconic structure linking Howrah and Kolkata over the Hooghly.

Belur Math – Headquarters of the Ramakrishna Mission, a place of peace and spirituality.

Rail Museum – Exhibits the history and heritage of Indian Railways in eastern India.

Vidyasagar Setu – One of the longest cable-stayed bridges in India, also called the Second Hooghly Bridge.

Botanical Garden – Home to a vast variety of exotic flora and the famous Great Banyan Tree.

Industrial Legacy

Howrah is known as the Sheffield of the East due to its historical foundries and engineering industries.

Festive Vibes

Festivals like Durga Puja are celebrated with grandeur, reflecting Howrah’s vibrant spirit.

The Riverside Charm

The ghats of Howrah offer peaceful views of the river and glimpses into daily life and rituals.
