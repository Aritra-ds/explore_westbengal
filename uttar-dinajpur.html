Explore Uttar Dinajpur

A scenic and culturally vibrant district of North Bengal.

History of Uttar Dinajpur

Uttar Dinajpur was carved out of the former West Dinajpur district and became an independent district in 1992. The region holds a rich cultural heritage and is known for its religious harmony and folk traditions.

With major rivers like Kulik and Nagar flowing through it, the district boasts natural beauty and agricultural abundance. The diverse communities living here contribute to a colorful and vibrant society.

Tourist Attractions

Kulik Bird Sanctuary – Asia's largest sanctuary for open-billed storks.

Raiganj Eco Park – A green retreat with boating and gardens.

Bahin Rajbari – An ancient palace with historical significance and beauty.

Kulik River – A scenic river with lush green surroundings.

Sri Krishna Avtar Baba Ramdev Mandir – A spiritual and peaceful temple.

Nature’s Serenity

Rolling fields, calm rivers, and birdsong define Uttar Dinajpur’s natural charm.

Heritage Highlights

Old temples and historical buildings narrate the district’s rich past.

Local Crafts

Skilled artisans create beautiful handmade items from jute and clay.
