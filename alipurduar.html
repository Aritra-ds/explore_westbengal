Explore Alipurduar

A paradise of forests, rivers, and wildlife in North Bengal.

History of Alipurduar

Alipurduar, carved out of Jalpaiguri in 2014, has quickly emerged as a vital district in North Bengal. Known for its lush forests and tribal heritage, the area once served as a key trade route between Assam and Bengal during the British era.

The district is home to vibrant cultural diversity, influenced by indigenous communities, and stands as a gateway to Bhutan and Northeast India, surrounded by pristine nature and wildlife.

Tourist Attractions

Buxa Fort – A historic fort amidst dense forests, once used by British to detain freedom fighters.

Rajabhatkhawa – A serene forest settlement ideal for eco-tourism and nature lovers.

Buxa Tiger Reserve – Rich in flora and fauna, a major sanctuary for Bengal tigers and more.

Chilapata Forest – A dense forest corridor with mysterious ruins and wild elephants.

Hatipota – A hidden tribal village known for indigenous culture and handcrafted bamboo art.

Untamed Wilderness

From tigers to elephants, Alipurduar is a hotspot for wildlife exploration and jungle safaris.

Local Handicrafts

Alipurduar’s traditional crafts, including bamboo and woodwork, reflect rich cultural heritage.

Natural Beauty

From lush forests to flowing rivers, Jalpaiguri offers breathtaking natural landscapes.
