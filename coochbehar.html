Explore Cooch Behar

The royal town of North Bengal known for its heritage and palaces.

History of Cooch Behar

Cooch Behar, once a princely state, has a rich and regal history deeply connected to the Koch dynasty. It was a major administrative and cultural center during the British Raj and later merged with the Indian Union in 1950.

Known for its unique Indo-Saracenic architecture and organized town planning, Cooch Behar stands as a symbol of elegance and royalty in North Bengal. Its legacy is preserved through its palaces, temples, and monuments.

Tourist Attractions

Cooch Behar Palace – Inspired by Buckingham Palace, it’s the pride of the district’s royal history.

Sagardighi – A scenic lake surrounded by government buildings and perfect for birdwatching.

Madan Mohan Temple – A sacred place for devotees, showcasing traditional architecture.

Baneswar Shiva Temple – An ancient temple surrounded by turtles, rich in folk legends and traditions.

Railway Heritage Museum – Preserving the transportation legacy of the region.

Natural Beauty

Coochbehar is known for its lush greenery, serene landscapes, and tranquil river views.

Festivals & Culture

Festivals like Ras Mela and Durga Puja are celebrated with devotion, drawing visitors from all around.

Lakes & Wildlife

Apart from history, the district charms with lakes, bird sanctuaries, and rural scenic beauty.
