History of Jhargram

Jhargram, located on the western edge of West Bengal, is known for its dense forests, tribal heritage, and royal history. The region once served as a princely state under British rule, with the royal Jhargram Palace still standing as a majestic reminder.

The district is rich in indigenous traditions and festivals, offering an authentic glimpse into the culture of various tribal communities. Jhargram is also a popular destination for nature lovers and eco-tourists.

Tourist Attractions

Jhargram Palace – A royal palace showcasing colonial architecture.

Kanak Durga Temple – A serene temple surrounded by forests and hills.

Ghagra Falls – A beautiful waterfall nestled within lush forests.

Kankrajhor – A tribal forested area rich in biodiversity and tranquility.

Chilkigarh Raj Palace – A historic structure symbolizing royal heritage.

Ancient Temples

Jhargram is home to several ancient temples showcasing intricate architectural styles.

Lush Forests

Dense sal forests and scenic landscapes make Jhargram a nature lover’s paradise.

Wildlife & Biodiversity

The region hosts rich wildlife including deer, elephants, and exotic birds.
